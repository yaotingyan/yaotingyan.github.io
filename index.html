About me

https://orcid.org/0000-0001-5574-0549

I'm Yaoting Yan, a Project Research Fellow working at National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (NAOJ).


My research focuses on star formation history and ongoing star formation, using two different tools: isotope ratios and ammonia masers.


Isotope abundance ratios provide a powerful tool to:
  • Trace stellar nucleosynthesis
  • Evaluate the composition of stellar ejecta
  • Constrain the chemical evolution of the Milky Way
Molecular maser lines:
  • Serve as signposts of high-mass star formation
  • Probe the excitation and kinematics of very compact regions in the close environment of young stellar objects
  • Provide useful targets for the measurements of trigonometric parallax and proper motions
